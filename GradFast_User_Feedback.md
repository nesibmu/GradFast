# GradFast User Feedback Notes

## Study snapshot
- Date(s): June 2026
- Number of users: 20
- Mix of class years: 6 first-years, 5 sophomores, 6 juniors, 3 seniors
- How the product was shown: live demo, screen share, and short self-serve walkthrough

## Questions asked
1. Did the generated plan look plausible for your school and major?
2. Was the plan easy to read?
3. Did the alternatives / transfer options feel useful?
4. Did the advisor chat and schedule edits make sense?
5. What felt strongest?
6. What felt weakest?
7. Would you use this for real planning?

## Individual notes

## Synthesis for README
- Common strengths: Users consistently liked the readable term-by-term structure, the ability to compare normal and accelerated timelines, and the conversational schedule edits. Several users said the product helped them think more strategically about graduation, internships, recruiting, study abroad, coterms, minors, and workload.
- Common weaknesses: Users wanted stronger grounding in official registrar data, prerequisite validation, course offering terms, transfer equivalencies, and transcript import. The most cautious users said they would use GradFast for exploration but still verify everything with an official degree audit.
- Most repeated request: Integrate official school catalogs, prerequisite data, and completed-course history so the generated plans feel more trustworthy.
- Best quote: “This helped me see graduation planning as a strategy problem instead of just a checklist.”
- Count who said it felt realistic: 16/20
- Count who liked the interactive edits: 15/20
- Count who wanted stronger registrar grounding: 12/20
